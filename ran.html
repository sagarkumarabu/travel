During the past few years, there has been a decline in the tiger population in Ranthambhore due to poaching and other reasons.[3] The number of tigers was 25 in 2005 and 48 in 2013.[4] As of 2014, there were 62 tigers in Ranthambore National Park.

Ecosystem valuation
Economic valuation of the tiger reserve estimated that its flow benefits are worth 8.3 billion rupees (0.56 lakh/hectare) annually.
Gene-pool protection services (7.11 billion), provisioning of water to the neighbouring region (115 million) and provisioning of habitat and refugia for wildlife (182 million)
were some of the important services that emanated from the tiger reserve.
Other services included nutrient cycling (34 million) and sequestration of carbon (69 million).
